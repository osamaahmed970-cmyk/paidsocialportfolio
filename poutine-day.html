01
The Foundation
Drive In-Store Traffic on National Poutine Day

Boardwalk Burgers wanted to capitalise on National Poutine Day (April 11) with a limited-time offer — $5 poutine instead of the regular $7.49 price — across 23 Canadian franchise locations. The goal was maximum in-store foot traffic within a tight 2-day window, with no e-commerce tracking available. Redemptions were tracked via POS system across all locations.

The campaign needed to work at two levels simultaneously: national brand awareness to drive broad reach, and hyper-local targeting to ensure each franchise location reached its immediate market. With a combined budget of $4,000 across four platforms, every dollar needed to work hard.

Rather than running one blanket national campaign, the strategy was to build a unique ad set per location on Meta — 22 individual ad sets, each geo-targeted around a specific franchise — while using Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube for broader national reach to amplify awareness at scale. Each platform was assigned a distinct role in the funnel.

03
Performance
4,500+ Redemptions in 48 Hours

Redemptions were tracked via POS system across all 23 locations. With 4,500+ confirmed in-store redemptions at the $5 promotional price point, the campaign generated $22,500 in direct trackable revenue on a $4,000 spend. Real basket value was almost certainly higher as most customers purchased additional items alongside the poutine.

Key Learnings
What This Campaign Taught Us
✅
Static Beats Video on CPC
Static ads consistently delivered lower cost per click than video formats. For short-window promotional campaigns where speed and cost efficiency matter, static should lead the creative strategy.
✅
Location-Level Ad Sets Drive Better Results
Running individual ad sets per franchise location — rather than one national campaign — allowed for precise geo-targeting and budget control. Winnipeg and Hamilton were top performers, a pattern worth leveraging in future flights.
💡
Align Objective to Goal
The campaign ran on an Engagement/Traffic objective but the real goal was in-store visits. For future campaigns, testing a Store Traffic or Conversions objective could reduce friction between click and redemption.
💡
Consider Advantage+ for Regional Scale
Several ad sets used narrow interest targeting. Enabling Advantage+ Audience on future campaigns could expand reach beyond defined interests while maintaining cost efficiency — worth A/B testing.
